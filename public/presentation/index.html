MLC Orientation

Identifying GBV Cases

GBV constitutes a medico-legal case requiring police notification under the Maldives DVPA (2012)

Physical Indicators

  • Injuries inconsistent with explanation
  • Bruising in defensive locations
  • Injuries at various healing stages
  • Burns, bite marks, pattern injuries
  • Genital or rectal trauma

Behavioural Indicators

  • Delayed presentation for care
  • Reluctance to undress/be examined
  • Evasive or inconsistent history
  • Companion answers for patient
  • Excessive anxiety, flinching
Screening Question (WHO): "In the past year, have you been hit, slapped, kicked, or otherwise physically hurt by someone?"

STI Prophylaxis & Emergency Contraception

STI Prophylaxis

Administer empirically ‚Äî do NOT wait for results

Gonorrhoea: Ceftriaxone 500 mg IM
Chlamydia: Azithromycin 1 g oral
Trichomoniasis: Metronidazole 2 g oral
Hepatitis B: HBV Vaccine ¬± HBIG
Tetanus: Td/Tdap if wound present

Emergency Contraception

Offer to ALL at risk of pregnancy

‚â§72h: Levonorgestrel 1.5 mg oral
‚â§120h: Ulipristal acetate 30 mg oral
‚â§5 days: Copper IUD insertion
EC does NOT protect against STIs. A pregnancy test should precede IUD insertion.

Best Practices

The LIVES Framework WHO

L

Listen

Listen closely with empathy, without judgment

"I'm here to listen. Please take your time."

I

Inquire

Ask about needs and concerns gently

"What are you most worried about right now?"

V

Validate

Affirm, believe, and remove self-blame

"What happened is NOT your fault. I believe you."

E

Enhance Safety

Collaboratively assess danger and plan safety

"Do you feel safe going home today?"

S

Support

Connect to services and ongoing support

"We can connect you to counselling. Would you like that?"

Objective Recording

Body Map Protocol

  • Use standardised anatomical diagrams
  • Mark each injury with: location, size (cm), shape, colour, type
  • Use clock positions for anogenital findings
  • Photograph with consent (scale ruler in frame)
  • Document ABSENCE of injury explicitly
  • Sign and date the body map

Mandatory Reporting & Patient Rights

Under the Maldives DVPA (2012): Healthcare providers MUST report all suspected/confirmed GBV cases to the Maldives Police Service, Family Protection Authority, and Ministry of Gender.

How to Explain Confidentiality Limits

"Everything you share with me is confidential. I will not share without your permission, except where the law requires me to report ‚Äî specifically, if there is an immediate threat to your life or safety, or if a child is involved. I will always inform you before any report is made."
